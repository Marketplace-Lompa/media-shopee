# E-commerce Visual Impact System

## Core Philosophy

> **An e-commerce image has exactly 1.5 seconds to stop the scroll. Every pixel must sell.**

E-commerce photography is not fashion photography. Fashion photography creates desire through aspiration and art. E-commerce photography creates **purchase confidence** through clarity, relatability, and strategic information delivery.

The buyer is asking 3 questions simultaneously:
1. **"What does it look like?"** → Clarity of the product
2. **"Will it look good on me?"** → Relatability of the model
3. **"Does it fit my life?"** → Context of the scenario

Every image in a listing must answer at least one of these questions.

---

## The 3-Shot System

Every product listing needs a minimum of **3 complementary shots** at different distances. Each distance serves a different psychological purpose.

### Shot 1: Wide / Full-Body (Hero Shot)

**Purpose:** First impression. Shows the complete outfit, silhouette, and context. This is the scroll-stopper.

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Framing** | Full body, head to feet, with breathing room above and below |
| **Model visibility** | 100% of the garment visible, no cropping on clothing |
| **Background** | Contextual — lifestyle setting that tells a story |
| **Pose** | Dynamic, in-motion or transitional — never static mannequin |
| **Proportion** | Model occupies 60-70% of frame height |

**Key instruction for the AI:**
```
Full-body shot showing the complete outfit from head to shoes. 
The model should be [description]. The entire garment is visible 
with no cropping on clothing edges. [Scenario]. Natural, 
mid-stride movement.
```

### Shot 2: Medium / Waist-Up (Detail Shot)

**Purpose:** Shows fit, fabric quality, and construction details. Builds confidence in product quality.

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Framing** | Waist-up or thigh-up, focus on the garment's key area |
| **Model visibility** | Torso and face, showing how the garment sits on the body |
| **Background** | Simplified — soft bokeh or clean context |
| **Pose** | Relaxed, natural — hands interacting with the garment casually |
| **Proportion** | Garment occupies 50-60% of frame |

**Key instruction for the AI:**
```
Medium shot from the waist up, showing how the [garment] fits 
the torso. Focus on the neckline, sleeve detail, and fabric texture. 
The model has a relaxed, natural expression. Soft background bokeh. 
Shot at approximately 50mm.
```

### Shot 3: Close-Up (Texture/Detail Shot)

**Purpose:** Shows fabric quality, texture, stitching, pattern detail. Eliminates "is it cheap?" doubt.

| Element | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Framing** | Tight crop on a specific garment detail |
| **Focus area** | Fabric texture, seam quality, pattern, button, label |
| **Background** | Garment itself is the background |
| **Pose** | Minimal — just the body part wearing the garment |
| **Proportion** | Detail occupies 80%+ of frame |

**Key instruction for the AI:**
```
Extreme close-up of the [specific detail: fabric texture / neckline 
construction / embroidery / button detail]. Shallow depth of field, 
macro-style focus showing individual [thread fibers / weave pattern / 
stitch quality]. The texture must feel tangible.
```

## High-Conversion Poses

### The Psychology of Poses

Poses communicate emotional states that influence purchase decisions.

| Pose Category | Emotional Signal | Conversion Impact |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| **Movement** | Energy, lifestyle, confidence | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Highest |
| **Casual Standing** | Approachability, everyday wear | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High |
| **Interactive** | Relatability, real-life use | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ High |
| **Seated** | Comfort, relaxed, lounge | ⭐⭐⭐ Medium |
| **Static Front-On** | Clinical, informational | ⭐⭐ Low |

### Movement Poses (Highest Conversion)

These create energy and show how the garment **lives** on a body in motion.

| Pose | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Mid-stride walk** | One foot ahead, arm swinging naturally, torso slightly rotated | Dresses, pants, full outfits |
| **Hair toss / wind catch** | Head turned, hair in motion, fabric responding to movement | Flowy garments, lightweight fabrics |
| **Turning away** | Body at 3/4 angle, looking back over shoulder | Back details, rear fit |
| **Stepping off curb** | One foot lower, dynamic shift, fabric in motion | Skirts, wide-leg pants |
| **Reaching for something** | Arm extended (hailing cab, reaching shelf), body elongated | Tops, jackets, sleeve details |

### Casual Standing Poses (High Conversion)

Natural, relaxed positions that feel unposed.

| Pose | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Weight on one hip** | Asymmetric stance, one hand in pocket or at side | Everything — the universal pose |
| **Arms crossed loosely** | Relaxed fold, not defensive, fingers visible | Jackets, blazers, layered looks |
| **Hand on bag strap** | Interacting with accessory, natural gesture | Complete outfits with accessories |
| **Leaning on wall** | Body diagonal, casual and relaxed | Casual wear, streetwear |
| **Looking down at phone** | Eyes down, natural modern gesture | Shows neckline and shoulder area |

### Interactive Poses (High Conversion)

Model interacting with environment — creates relatable "I can see myself doing this" scenarios.

| Pose | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Holding coffee cup** | Two hands on cup, slight warmth-seeking gesture | Cozy knitwear, winter pieces |
| **Adjusting sunglasses** | Hand at temple, casual glamour | Summer pieces, casual chic |
| **Walking with shopping bags** | In motion with bags in hand, urban lifestyle | Complete outfits, everyday wear |
| **Sitting on steps** | Casual seated, legs visible, urban setting | Pants, skirts, shoes |
| **Checking reflection** | Looking at window/mirror, natural self-assessment | Shows how garment looks in movement |

### Seated Poses (Medium Conversion)

| Pose | Description | Best For |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Cross-legged on couch** | Relaxed, informal, home setting | Loungewear, homewear, comfy pieces |
| **Perched on stool** | Upright seated, legs visible, studio-ish | Pants fit, leg-showing pieces |
| **Café table** | Seated with forearms on table, social setting | Tops, neckline details |

## Camera Angles for Maximum Impact

### Primary Angles

| Angle | Effect | When to Use |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Eye level** | Neutral, natural, relatable | Default for most shots |
| **Slightly below eye level** | Empowering, elongating, aspirational | Hero shots, full-body |
| **Slightly above** | Approachable, intimate, friendly | Seated poses, lifestyle |
| **3/4 body angle** | Shows dimension, slimming, dynamic | Almost always better than straight-on |
| **Profile / Side** | Shows silhouette, structure, draping | Structured garments, layered outfits |

### Angle Rules

1. **Never shoot straight-on at 0°** — it flattens the garment and looks clinical
2. **Default to 15-30° rotation** — 3/4 angle adds dimension to everything
3. **Alternate sides between shots** — create visual variety in listing
4. **Low angle for hero shot** — makes the model (and garment) look powerful
5. **Eye level for detail shots** — honest, informative, no distortion

## Brazilian Model Framework

### Diversity as Default

Brazilian e-commerce photography must reflect Brazil's ethnic diversity. Never default to a single phenotype.

### Model Description Building Blocks

Build model descriptions by combining these elements:

#### Skin Tone Spectrum
```
"light olive skin" | "warm brown skin" | "deep dark skin" | 
"golden tanned skin" | "fair with warm undertones" | 
"rich melanin-deep skin"
```

#### Hair Types and Styles
```
"natural curly afro hair" | "long straight dark hair" | 
"wavy shoulder-length brown hair" | "short textured coils" | 
"braided hair with loose ends" | "messy beach waves" | 
"tight coils pulled into a high bun"
```

#### Age Ranges
```
"young woman in her early 20s" | "woman in her mid 30s" | 
"mature woman in her 50s" | "young man in his late 20s"
```

#### Body Types
```
"athletic build" | "curvy figure" | "slim frame" | 
"plus-size" | "petite" | "tall and lean"
```

### Complete Model Description Template
```
A [age range] Brazilian [gender] with [skin tone], [hair type 
and style], [body type], [expression — relaxed/confident/joyful].
```

## Visual Impact Techniques

### Seasonal & Contextual Styling Intelligence (CRITICAL)
Before selecting the scenario or the secondary clothing pieces (bottoms, shoes, accessories), **analyze the primary garment's season and weather profile**. The entire image ecology must make logical sense.

| Garment Season | Styling Action | Scenario Action |
|----------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Heavy Winter** (Thick knits, coats, ponchos) | **Must** pair with cold-weather items: leather pants, dark jeans, boots, turtlenecks underneath. NEVER bare legs, shorts, or sandals. | Muted lighting, overcast days, cozy indoor cafés, cool-toned urban streets (Gramado/Curitiba vibe). |
| **Mid-Season / Autumn** (Light cardigans, long sleeves) | Ankle boots, closed shoes, midi skirts, light denim. | Golden hour, falling leaves, late afternoon urban settings. |
| **High Summer** (Cropped tops, shorts, light dresses) | Bare legs, sandals, light accessories. | Bright midday sun, beaches, tropical parks, vibrant outdoor markets. |

> **If the AI contradicts the season** (e.g., placing a heavy winter poncho with a mini-skirt and bare legs in a sunny summer setting), **the visual impact is destroyed** because it looks unnatural to the buyer's context.

### The Scroll-Stopper Formula

The first image in a listing must contain **at least 3** of these elements:

1. **Color contrast** — garment pops against background
2. **Movement energy** — something is in motion (hair, fabric, stride)
3. **Emotional expression** — face communicates a feeling
4. **Depth layers** — foreground, subject, background are distinct
5. **Aspirational context** — setting the buyer wants to be in

### Color Strategy for Product Visibility

| Garment Color | Best Background | Avoid |
|---------------|----------------|-------|
| **White/cream** | Dark or saturated backgrounds | White walls, bright sand |
| **Black** | Light, airy backgrounds | Dark rooms, night scenes |
| **Pastels** | Neutral warm tones, natural settings | Other pastels (gets lost) |
| **Bright/saturated** | Clean, minimal backgrounds | Competing bright colors |
| **Earth tones** | Urban or green natural settings | Brown/beige backgrounds |
| **Prints/patterns** | Solid, simple backgrounds | Busy or patterned settings |

### Composition Rules for Marketplace Thumbnails

Marketplace listings display as **small square thumbnails**. Optimize for this:

1. **Subject centered or slightly off-center** — no extreme rule-of-thirds
2. **Garment fully visible even at thumbnail size** — no extreme crops
3. **High contrast between garment and background** — must read at 200x200px
4. **Simple, uncluttered composition** — one clear focal point
5. **Face visible** — listings with visible faces convert 15-20% higher

## Listing Set Composition Strategy

### The Optimal 5-Image Listing Set

| Image # | Shot Type | Purpose | Priority |
|---------|-----------|---------|----------|
| **1** | Wide / Hero | Scroll-stopper. Full outfit + scenario + movement | 🔴 Critical |
| **2** | Medium | Fit and fabric detail. Waist-up, natural pose | 🔴 Critical |
| **3** | Back View | Rear fit, closure details, complete picture | 🟡 Important |
| **4** | Close-Up | Texture, quality, construction proof | 🟡 Important |
| **5** | Lifestyle / Action | Model in use context, relatable scenario | 🟢 Bonus |

### Variation Across the Set

To keep the listing visually engaging, vary these between shots:

| Element | Shot 1 | Shot 2 | Shot 3 | Shot 4 | Shot 5 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Distance** | Wide | Medium | Wide (back) | Close | Medium |
| **Angle** | 3/4 left | Front slight | 3/4 right | Macro | Profile |
| **Pose** | Walking | Standing casual | Turning | Detail only | Interactive |
| **Background** | Outdoor | Soft bokeh | Same as 1 | Garment only | Indoor/café |

### Consistency Rules Within a Set

While varying shots, keep these **consistent** across all images:

- Same model (identity consistency)
- Same garment (obviously)
- Compatible color grading (same "mood family")
- Similar time of day / lighting temperature
- Same resolution and aspect ratio
